III.—-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

        Observing the rule of thirds:

Top third: blue

Bottom two thirds: green, flecked with bits of white- not snow. It’s summertime; these are dandelions;

        May is occasionally cooperative, and luckily, today was one of those days. Grandpa always said he wanted to be buried under that live oak near the creek behind the old house in Tellico Plains. The current landowner was willing to oblige this request.

        “And that was real sweet of him to do that,” said Grammaw. Everyone agreed, nodding and murmuring. We were all standing in the tall grass next to the tree, cookies-and-cream dippin’ dots around a dead body. It’s a strange thing to do in life, this whole arrangement- thankfully, we only do it once per person.

        I looked from side to side at my brothers. We squirmed in our itchy cotton suits, now covered in a fine layer of pollen, dandruff, and dandelion fuzz. There were sweat bees and damselflies hovering at our ankles. Matthew tugged at his collar as it chafed against his skin in the rising noonday heat. I looked across the circle at my mother and aunt standing reverently still like lawn ornaments, listening to the drawl of the pastor as he read from Ephesians 4:

- “There is one body, and one spirit, even as ye are called in one hope of your calling: one Lord, one faith, one baptism, one God and father of all, who is above all and through all and in you all.”

        I wish I could be a lawn ornament. It’s gotta be calming in a certain way. I shove my restless hands in my pockets. My knuckles graze something…

Oh cool, a coin. I needed that.

V.—---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

People go on and on about the Soul- if it’s real, what it is, where in the body it’s stored, etc. But the truth is pretty straightforward- the human Soul is visible with infrared goggles. If you can’t see it with the goggles, it’s not there anymore. That’s how they knew Grandpa was ready to be put in the box. The coroner flips down his infrared visor- he’s looking for blues and greens from head to toe. Large patches of red or orange mean “Hold the fort! Take the toe tag off! Wheel him out of here! He’s still kicking!” On the other hand, yellow indicates that the Soul is currently being judged. It’s not likely to be sent back into the body at this point, but there have been a few known cases, so it’s best to leave the subject on the table and come back to it after lunch.

        Most cases of live burial are the result of morticians conducting Soul-exams with faulty technology. It’s best to get your infrared kit serviced every 24-30 months. As a funeral home, there’s nothing worse for your business than claw marks on the inside of a box- though in fairness, these incidents almost always go unnoticed.
